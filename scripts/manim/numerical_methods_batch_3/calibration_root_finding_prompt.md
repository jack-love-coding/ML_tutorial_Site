## Stage 1 — ConceptAnalyzer

The root teaching question is: how do bisection, Newton, and secant iterations use different information to find the same zero, and what must be checked before trusting their output? The conceptual chain is “turn a target into a signed residual, establish existence and uniqueness, see a guaranteed bracket method, see a derivative-based open method, see a function-only open method, compare update and evaluation costs, inspect failure contracts, and hand off from zeros to minimization.” The video must show that algorithms are not ranked by iteration count alone. A Newton update consumes a derivative; bisection maintains a root-containing interval; secant estimates slope from two function values. Reliability, local speed, and evaluation cost are separate dimensions.

Keep the residual’s meaning visible. If F(b) is negative, the mean sigmoid probability is below 0.62; if positive, it is above 0.62. Because every sigmoid term increases with b, the mean and F increase with b. At `b=-4`, F is negative; at `b=4`, F is positive. Continuity plus endpoint sign change establishes existence inside the interval, while strict monotonicity establishes uniqueness. Do not claim endpoint sign change alone establishes uniqueness. Do not use a general root icon without showing the curve’s crossing and the sign context.
